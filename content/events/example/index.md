abstract: |
  The primary mechanism driving Neural Language Models (NLMs) is attention. This mechanism enables these models to assign varying levels of importance to different words within a sentence, effectively allowing the model to focus on the most critical elements for understanding and generating language. In our study, we explored the parallels between neural attention and human attention. We sought to determine how closely neural attention mirrors human attentional patterns, examined the feasibility of tuning NLMs to align with human-like attention, and assessed the consequent effects on the models' overall performance. Our findings provide insights into the similarities and differences between human and neural attention mechanisms, and offer potential ways to improve the interpretability and efficiency of neural language models by incorporating more human-like attention strategies.
